Forest Park Trailheads

Below are three maps that show the Hoyt Arboretum, Wildwood Trail, and Leif Erikson access points in Forest Park, Portland, Oregon. These are all great trails and areas to explore in the Forest Park area for individuals who are new to the area or wish to get outdoors more.

Hoyt Arboretum:

The Hoyt Arboretum is well-known for the wide varieties of trees and trails throughout the area. There are many trails the intersect this area, including Wildwood Trail. The Zoo, Japanese Gardens, and many other gardens are within short reach of this area as well, making it a good starting point for anyone looking to get outside in Portland.

Wildwood Trail:

Wildwood Trail is over 30 miles long and touches many other trails in Forest Park. It is a great place to start for someone who wants to get to know different areas of Forest Park better. The trail does have some inclines (and declines), making it a great trail for hikers of all levels.

Leif Erikson Trail:

Leif Erikson Trail is over 10 miles long and is one of the few trails in Forest Park that is bike (and horse!) friendly. Many other trails branch off of Leif Erikson as well, making this another great trail for beginners who are new to hiking or individuals who want to explore Forest Park.
